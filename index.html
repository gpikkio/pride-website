Planetary Radio Interferometry and Doppler Experiment

Precision spacecraft tracking for planetary science using Earth-based Very Long Baseline Interferometry networks.

Maximizing Mission Science

PRIDE is a versatile tracking technique that extracts high-precision state vectors from spacecraft. It operates without requiring dedicated onboard hardware, relying solely on standard communication signals.

By observing the spacecraft's radio transmission alongside distant celestial sources, PRIDE achieves sub-nanoradian lateral positioning and millihertz radial velocity resolution.

Scientific Methodology

PRIDE relies on two distinct observables derived from the spacecraft's radio downlink, providing critical data for orbit determination and dynamical modelling.

Near-Field VLBI

Multiple radio telescopes simultaneously record the spacecraft signal. By nodding between the target and a phase-reference calibrator, PRIDE measures the spacecraft's lateral position within the International Celestial Reference Frame (ICRF).

Doppler Shift Analysis

High-resolution spectral processing of the narrow-band carrier signal extracts frequency deviations. This provides ultra-precise radial velocity estimates, complementing standard closed-loop tracking methods.

PRIDE on the JUICE Mission

PRIDE is an official science experiment for ESA's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (JUICE). It provides independent tracking data to refine the ephemerides of the Jovian system.

These observables are vital for characterising the dynamics of Ganymede, Callisto, and Europa, offering insights into tidal dissipation and the internal structure of these icy worlds.

Mission Heritage

Huygens Probe

Enabled the reconstruction of the descent trajectory through the atmosphere of Saturn's moon, Titan, validating the near-field tracking technique.

Mars Express

Demonstrated high-precision astrometry during the Phobos flyby, providing complementary data for mass and ephemeris estimation.

Venus Express

Conducted multi-station tracking for high-resolution radio occultations, probing the thermal structure of the Venusian atmosphere.

Interplanetary Plasma Diagnostics

Radio signals from deep-space probes traverse the interplanetary medium. This propagation induces measurable phase fluctuations in the received signal.

By analysing these scintillations, PRIDE estimates the Total Electron Content (TEC) of the solar wind, providing valuable data for studying solar wind turbulence and monitoring space weather.
